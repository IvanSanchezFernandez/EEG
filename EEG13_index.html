SUMMARY

After reviewing this post, you have solidified some of the normal and abnormal EEG features that you learned in prior posts.


In particular, you have seen that slowing can be focal or generalized (or both at the same time). Generalized slowing is suggestive of a diffuse encephalopathy of non-specific etiology. Focal slowing is suggestive of a functional or structural lesion in the region affected by the slowing.


You also learned about OIRDA (what does OIRDA stands for?) and the association of OIRDA with a frequent type of childhood epilepsy (in what childhood epilepsy does OIRDA appear?). As another type of "special slowing", you learned about posterior slow waves of youth (a normal variant seen in youth). In addition, you are able to localize epileptiform discharges better, even when they appear at the "end of chain".

Keep learning!
